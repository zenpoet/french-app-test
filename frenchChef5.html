Chef Interview at MarieBette Cafe

What motivated you to become a baker?

(Patrick) I was working with an artist and we travelled to Spain for a show that he did. We went to this amazing restaurant in Galicia, Spain and there was this 10 course Michelin star meal we had. I always wanted to go to culinary school, but that’s what inspired me to enroll in culinary school in New York.

(Jason) I went to College for pre-med and went to France for a semester. Then I decided that I didn’t want to be stuck in school for 4 years. I wanted a job where I could travel, so I started working in advertising, thinking it would be a creative field, but it wasn’t very fulfilling for me. What I really wanted was to have my own business one day. I really enjoy cooking and entertaining, so I thought: why not go to culinary school?

What is a typical day for you?

(Patrick) During the week, I try to be at the bakery at 4:00am. It gives me enough time for the croissants to proof and just to bake all the products before 7:00am, and then I am baking the bread throughout the morning and preparing for the next day. This is a 10 to 12 hour job, seven days a week. Eventually, we will have a day off!

How do you make chocolate croissants?

Flour, water, milk, yeast, salt, sugar, butter, egg wash on top. The process takes a few days, because we ferment the flour overnight, day 2 we make the dough and let it sit overnight, day 3 we make the croissants and day 4 we bake them.
