Chef Interview at Restaurant Pomme

Emma: Do you speak French?

Geraldin: Yes, just moved back from France in July. I was born in the states, but lived in France for twenty years. I met my husband in the states but he's more French than I am. He grew up there.

Annbel: What is your restaurant French connection?

Geraldin: The connection is my father - he owns the restaurant and I work with him and he has been here in the US for 50 years.

Emma: What drew you to the restaurant business?

Geraldin: I have been in the restaurant business all my life. My dad is a master chef in France, so I have always known I wanted to be in that sort of environment. I went to college in Rhode Island for restaurant management, then I went to France for twenty years and then I came back to help my dad.

Annbel: When did you first consider yourself a chef?

I first considered myself a chef when I was like eight because I used to love watch my dad - to me it was an art.

Classic Butternut Squash Soup With a Hint of Apples

Serves: 4

Ingredients

    Ingredients in English

  • 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 carrot, diced
  • 1 celery stalk, diced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 4 cups cubed butternut squash
  • ½ teaspoon fine sea salt
  • 4 cups chicken broth (low sodium)
  • ½ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 sweet apples
  • ½ teaspoon chopped thyme

    Ingredients in French

  • 2 cuillères à soupe d'huile d’olive
  • 1 carotte, coupée
  • 1 tige de céleri
  • 1 oignon, coupée
  • 4 tasses de courge musquée
  • ½ petite cuillère de sel
  • 4 tasses de bouillon de poulet
  • ½ petite cuillère de poivre noir
  • 1 tasse de crème
  • 2 pommes
  • ½ petite cuillère du thym

Directions

    CHAUFFER heat oil in large soup pot
    AJOUTER: add carrot, celery, apples and onion
    FAIRE CUIRE: cook until vegetables have begun to soften and onion turns translucent (3-4 minutes)
    MÉLANGER: stir in butternut squash, thyme, chicken broth, salt, and pepper
    FAIRE BOULLIR: bring to a boil
    RÉDUIRE: reduce heat
    MIJOUTER: simmer until squash is fork-tender
    RÉDUIRE EN PURÉE: purée soup in blender
    AJOUTER: add heavy cream while blending

Potatoes Dauphinoise--French Gratin Potatoes

Serves: 4

Ingredients

    Ingredients in English

  • 3 lbs of waxy potatoes
  • 6 ounces Gruyere cheese
  • ¾ pint double cream
  • Black pepper
  • 2-4 garlic cloves
  • 1 ounce butter
  • Cheese for topping (optional)
  • Salt

    Ingredients in French

  • Trois livres de pommes de terre
  • Six ounces de Gruyère
  • 3/4 de crème
  • Poivre noir
  • Deux-quatre gousses d’ail
  • Une once de beurre
  • Fromage (optionnel)
  • Sel

Directions

    PRÈCHAUFER: preheat the oven to 300f/150c
    COUPER: slice the potatoes
    GRAISSER: take a heavy-bottomed dish or an ovenproof gratin dish and grease it with some of the butter
    DISPOSER EN PLUSIEURS OUCHES: layer potato slices in dish, slightly overlapping each other
    ASSAISONER: season lightly with salt and pepper
    METTRE: put the cream and the garlic into a pan and bring to boil
    VERSER: pour over potatoes
    PARSMER: dot the potatoes with the rest of the butter
    CUIRE: bake for 1hr to 1 ½ hrs, or until potatoes are soft
    SERVIR: serve hot

Poulet Géraldine

Serves: 4

Ingredients

    Ingredients in English

  • 4 skinless, boneless, chicken breasts
  • All-purpose flour, for dredging
  • Kosher salt
  • Ground black pepper
  • 4 large eggs
  • 3 tablespoons water
  • ¼ cup extra-virgin olive oil
  • ½ lemon, with rind, cut in thin rounds
  • ½ cup dry white wine (Pinot Grigio)
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • ½ lemon juiced
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter

    Ingredients in French

  • 4 blancs de poulet
  • farine
  • sel
  • Poivre noir moulu
  • 4 oeufs
  • 3 cuillères à soupe d’eau
  • ¼ tasses d'huile d’olive
  • ½ citron
  • ½ tasse de vin blanc
  • 1 tasse de bouillon de poulet
  • ½ citron, jus
  • 2 cuillères à soupe de beurre
  • ½ tasse de persil

Directions

    METTRE: Put the chicken breasts side by side on a cutting board and lay piece of plastic wrap over them.
    ECRASER: Pound the chicken breasts with a flat meat mallet until they are about ¼ inch thick
    METTRE: Put some flour in a shallow platter
    ASSAISONER: season with a fair amount of salt and pepper
    MELANGER: Mix with a fork to distribute evenly
    FOUETTER: In a wide bowl, beat the eggs with 3 tablespoons of water to make an egg wash
    CHAUFFER:Heat oil over medium-high flame in a large skillet
    SAUPOUDRER: Dredge both sides of the chicken cutlets in the seasoned flour
    TREMPER: then dip in the egg wash to coat completely, letting the excess drip off
    FRIRE: When oil is hot, add cutlets and fry for 2 min on each side until golden brown
    METTRE: Put cutlets on a large platter in a single layer to keep warm
    CUIRE: Cook lemon slices in pan for 1-2 minutes, until fragrant
    AJOUTER: Add wine, broth and lemon juice
    MIJOUTER: Simmer for 5 minutes to reduce sauce slightly
    CUISINER: Grill the butter in some flour and add it to the skillet, this will thicken the sauce
    MÉLANGER: Stir to incorporate and dissolve the flour
    RÉDUIRE: Reduce the heat to medium-low and return chicken to pan
    PLACER: Place the lemon slices on top of the cutlets
    MIJOUTER: Simmer gently for 2 minutes to heat chicken through
    ASSAISONER: Season with salt and pepper and garnish with chopped parsley before serving
