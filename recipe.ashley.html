Potato Dauphinoise or Potato Gratin

Serves: 4

Ingredients

  • 2 cup heavy cream or half and half
  • 1 large clove garlic, smashed
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 sprig of thyme
  • 2 lbs baking potatoes, sliced paper thin
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • Freshly grated nutmeg to taste
  • 1⁄2 - 3⁄4 cup grated Gruyère Cheese
  • 2 tass de crème riche en matière grasse ou moitié-moitié
  • 1 grand gousse d'ail, écrasée
  • 1 feuille de laurier
  • 1 brin de thym
  • 2 pounds de pommes de terre
  • sel et poivre
  • noix de muscade
  • ½ - ¾ tasse de Gruyère râpé fromage

Directions

MÉLANGER: In a small saucepan, combine the cream, half of the garlic clove, garlic and, bay leaf.

BOUILLIR: Bring just to a boil. Remove from the heat and let the flavors infuse as you assemble the gratin.

PRÉCHAUFFER:Heat the oven to 350°F. Butter a 2-qt. gratin dish and rub the dish with the other half of the garlic clove.

CHEVAUCHER: Overlapping slightly spread the potatoes in the bottom of the gratin dish,

VERSER: Pour 1/3 cup of the cream over the potatoes, season with salt and pepper. Repeat layers 2 times.

ASSAISONNER: Season with nutmeg. Sprinkle with 1⁄2 - 3/4 cup Gruyère Cheese.

CUIRE: Bake for 45 minutes to 1 hour until the potatoes are very tender when pierced with a knife.

LAISSER REFROIDIR: Let cool for about 10 minutes before serving.
