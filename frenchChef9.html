Interview with Ashley Clarke

What is your French Connection?

(Ashley) I was trained in French cooking and went to France four times

What got you interested in French cuisine and cooking?

(Ashley) I was interested in French cooking because I love food and my mom cooked a lot. I majored in French Cuisine in Culinary school.

How long have you been cooking?

(Ashley) I have been cooking for 10 years.

What training do you have?

(Ashley) I was trained in French, Italian, and Thai cuisine.

What is your favorite dish?

(Ashley) My favroite dish is Tarte Tatin, an upside down apple pie.

Who is your inspiration?

(Ashley) My mom or Grandma, they cooked a lot and entertained for very large groups of people.

What is the hardest dish you have ever made, and why?

(Ashley) Puff pastry because it is layers and layers of folded butter and it takes three days to make. Also it is butter so if you don’t keep it at the right temperature it will melt in your hands.

Potato Dauphinoise or Potato Gratin

Serves: 4

Ingredients

  • 2 cup heavy cream or half and half
  • 1 large clove garlic, smashed
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 sprig of thyme
  • 2 lbs baking potatoes, sliced paper thin
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • Freshly grated nutmeg to taste
  • 1⁄2 - 3⁄4 cup grated Gruyère Cheese
  • 2 tass de crème riche en matière grasse ou moitié-moitié
  • 1 grand gousse d'ail, écrasée
  • 1 feuille de laurier
  • 1 brin de thym
  • 2 pounds de pommes de terre
  • sel et poivre
  • noix de muscade
  • ½ - ¾ tasse de Gruyère râpé fromage

Directions

MÉLANGER: In a small saucepan, combine the cream, half of the garlic clove, garlic and, bay leaf.

BOUILLIR: Bring just to a boil. Remove from the heat and let the flavors infuse as you assemble the gratin.

PRÉCHAUFFER:Heat the oven to 350°F. Butter a 2-qt. gratin dish and rub the dish with the other half of the garlic clove.

CHEVAUCHER: Overlapping slightly spread the potatoes in the bottom of the gratin dish,

VERSER: Pour 1/3 cup of the cream over the potatoes, season with salt and pepper. Repeat layers 2 times.

ASSAISONNER: Season with nutmeg. Sprinkle with 1⁄2 - 3/4 cup Gruyère Cheese.

CUIRE: Bake for 45 minutes to 1 hour until the potatoes are very tender when pierced with a knife.

LAISSER REFROIDIR: Let cool for about 10 minutes before serving.

What are some French speaking countries that you have been too?

(Ashley) I have been to Vietnam, the food is really good, and they have really good French pastries. Since it is cheap, you can get really nice pastry for twenty-five cents or less when the cost would normally be four dollars.
