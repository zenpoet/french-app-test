Chef Interview at Fleurie Restaurant

What is the most unusual food you have ever had?

(General Manager, Joseph Scala) I have had cricket tacos. I’ve also eaten brains. Calf and lamb brains. Duck tongue too. It isn’t that adventurous, it is supposed to be a delicacy. Also, I’ve had monkfish liver. There are a lot of nutrients in the liver.

Who is your inspiration?

(Chef Brian Helleberg) Jean Yves Schillinger who is a chef and is from Alsace, France. He has a 3 michelin star restaurant and I worked for him and learned all sorts of recipes and techniques.

What is your favorite French speaking place to visit? Why?

(Beverage Director, Erin Scala) Paris, because it is such a magical place.

CRÈME BRÛLÉE

Serves: 1

Ingredients

  • 1 qt cream
  • 200 g sugar
  • 2 vanilla beans
  • 8 egg yolks
  • 1 quart de crème
  • 200g. de sucre
  • 2 gousses de vanille
  • 8 jaunes d'oeufs

Directions

COUPER. Cut open and scrape vanilla beans. AJOUTER. Add cream and bring to simmer. LAISSER. Let stand 10-20 minutes. FOUETTER. Whisk sugar and egg yolks until pale, and slowly incorporate warm cream into yolks. CUIRE. Pour brûlée into cups and place cups in a water bath to bake at 325 degrees F for 40 min (ensure cups are not completely submerged in the water).

What is your French connection?

(Chef Brian Helleberg) I am American, but studied and worked with a French chef where I learned recipes, ideas, and French techniques. I brought his teachings back to Charlottesville and have continued on with them.
