Chef Interview at Aromas Cafe

What is your French connection?

Born in Morocco, Raised in France, Canadian citizen and proud American.

Is any of your family in the food services industry?

All my family is in the food services industry. My brother Paul used to have one of the biggest resturants in Monteal. My other brother moved to South Africa and opened a restaurant called Carbon Café.

What is your favorite dish?

Cooking lamb. Braised slowly and could be served different ways over risotto or over couscous. That is my favorite dish

How do you recognize a good chef when you are looking for staff?

There’s a lot of different ways. The education, the school, the place where he did work, but I always give people the chance to learn. And and give him onions, see how he’s going to cut them.

And make an omelet which means, if you’ve seen the movie The Hundred-Foot Journey, remember when he make her the omelet and she hires him? It’s true in France if you don’t know how to make an omelet they won’t hire you

Moroccan Meatballs

Serves: 4

Ingredients

    For the Meatballs:

  • 1 lb lean beef
  • Teaspoon salt
  • Teaspoon ground pepper
  • Teaspoon cumin
  • Teaspoon granulated garlic
  • Teaspoon paprika
  • Teaspoon cinnamon
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • Chopped parsley (optional)

    • For the Tomatoes:

  • 3 fresh and ripe tomatoes (diced)
  • ½ onion (chopped)
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • Teaspoon salt
  • Teaspoon ground pepper
  • Teaspoon cumin
  • Teaspoon granulated garlic
  • Teaspoon paprika
  • Teaspoon cinnamon
  • 4 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 3 skinned potatoes
  • Chopped parsley/chopped basil
  • 1-cup water


  • 1 livre boeuf maigre
  • Une petite cuillère sel
  • Une petite cuillère poivre haché
  • Une petite cuillère cumin
  • Une petite cuillère ail grenelé
  • Une petite cuillère paprika
  • Une petite cuillère cannelle
  • 2 petite cuillère huile d’olive
  • persil émincé (en option)



  • 3 tomates frais et mûr (dés)
  • ½ oignon (émincé)
  • 2 gousse d’ail
  • Une petite cuillère sel
  • Une petite cuillère poivre haché
  • Une petite cuillère cumin
  • Une petite cuillère ail grenelé
  • Une petite cuillère paprika
  • Une petite cuillère cannelle
  • 4 petite cuillère huile d’olive
  • 3 desquamé pommes des terres
  • persil émincé/basilic émincé
  • Un coup d'eau

Directions

Melanger all the tomato’s ingredients together, except for potatoes, water and olive oil. Put aside. Put all the meatball ingredients together and shape into a sphere. Put aside. Put the tomato mixture into a Dutch Pan with the 4 tbs olive oil on high heat until tomatoes start to dissolve. Turn heat to low, add meatballs and cover for 5 minutes or until meatballs are of color. And the potatoes and cover until potatoes are soft. Garnish with the parsley or basil. Serve and enjoy.

What is the funniest or weirdest expirence you've ever had with a costumer?

The funniest and weirdest one I will say was in France. I used to work for a 4 star hotel and restaurant, high class and very strict. There was one customer. I still remember her name, she was from a very wealty family and she used to sit down and, so nobody sat near her, pay for the whole row of tables. She even had a poodle and I was assigned to wait on her and feed her dog. I was young, and one day she invited me to some seminar she wanted to give at the hotel and I prefered to go dancing with my friend so I didn't go. The next day I came, she had her cane and she got so upset she hit me on the head with the cane. It's true I still remember this one. She hit me one time and I said something like "oh", and she said she was insulted I didn't come to the seminar. I said I was sorry, but when I came to the table she was still upset and she hit me again with the cane. The directir of the hotel came and he punished me for two weeks. I washed dishes. That is the funniest and weirdest experience.
